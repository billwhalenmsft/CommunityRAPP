# Contoso Corporation — Performance Review Process
**Document:** HR-POL-005  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Owner:** Talent Management, Human Resources

---

## Overview

Contoso's performance review process is designed to support employee development, align individual goals to company priorities, and recognize outstanding contributions. Reviews are **annual** with a mid-year check-in.

---

## 1. Review Calendar

| Milestone | Timing |
|---|---|
| Goal Setting (new year or new hire) | January 1–31 |
| Mid-Year Check-In (manager + employee) | July 1–31 |
| Self-Assessment Opens | October 1 |
| Self-Assessment Due | October 31 |
| Manager Review Draft Due | November 15 |
| Calibration Meetings | November 17–30 |
| Reviews Finalized & Shared with Employees | December 10 |
| Merit Increase Effective Date | March 1 (following year) |

---

## 2. Rating Scale

Contoso uses a **5-point rating scale**:

| Rating | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | **Exceptional** | Consistently exceeds all expectations; top contributor; sets the standard |
| 4 | **Exceeds Expectations** | Frequently surpasses goals; strong performer with notable impact |
| 3 | **Meets Expectations** | Fully achieves goals; reliable and competent contributor |
| 2 | **Needs Improvement** | Partially meets expectations; a development plan is required |
| 1 | **Unsatisfactory** | Fails to meet core expectations; immediate improvement required |

---

## 3. Goal Setting

### 3.1 SMART Goals
All goals should be **Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-Bound**.

### 3.2 Goal Categories
Employees set **3–5 goals** annually across two categories:

| Category | Weight |
|---|---|
| Business/Functional Goals | 70% |
| Growth & Development Goals | 30% |

### 3.3 Goal Entry
Goals are entered and tracked in **Workday > Performance > Goals**. Manager approval required by January 31.

---

## 4. Mid-Year Check-In

The mid-year check-in (July) is an informal, non-rated conversation to:
- Review progress toward goals
- Adjust goals if business priorities have shifted
- Identify development needs and coaching opportunities
- Surface any concerns early

Managers must complete mid-year check-ins in Workday by July 31.

---

## 5. Self-Assessment

In October, employees complete a self-assessment in Workday that includes:

1. **Goal Progress** – Rate your own progress on each goal (1–5)
2. **Key Accomplishments** – Describe 2–3 significant contributions this year
3. **Challenges** – Describe any obstacles encountered and how they were addressed
4. **Development** – What skills or capabilities did you grow this year?
5. **Looking Ahead** – What are your goals for next year and your career aspirations?

Self-assessments should be thoughtful and specific. Aim for 300–500 words total.

---

## 6. Manager Review

Managers complete the performance review in Workday by November 15. The review includes:

- **Overall Rating** (1–5)
- **Rating Rationale** – Minimum 200 words explaining the rating with specific examples
- **Goal Ratings** – Individual rating on each goal
- **Strengths** – 2–3 specific behavioral strengths
- **Development Areas** – 1–2 areas for growth with suggested actions
- **Recommended Action** (for ratings of 1 or 2): Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

**Calibration:** All manager ratings are reviewed in calibration sessions with HR Business Partners to ensure fairness and consistency across teams.

---

## 7. Review Conversation

After calibration, managers share the finalized review with employees by **December 10**. This conversation should:

- Be private, scheduled in advance (30–60 minutes)
- Allow the employee to respond and ask questions
- Set the stage for next year's goal planning

Employees may submit a written rebuttal within 10 business days if they disagree with their rating. Rebuttals are reviewed by the HR Business Partner.

---

## 8. Merit Increases

Annual merit increases are effective **March 1** and are directly tied to performance ratings:

| Rating | Merit Increase Range |
|---|---|
| 5 – Exceptional | 6–8% |
| 4 – Exceeds Expectations | 4–6% |
| 3 – Meets Expectations | 2–4% |
| 2 – Needs Improvement | 0–1% |
| 1 – Unsatisfactory | 0% |

Increases are subject to budget availability and may be adjusted by HR and Finance.

---

## 9. Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)

Employees receiving a rating of 1 or 2 will be placed on a **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)**:

- Duration: Typically **60–90 days**
- Content: Specific, measurable improvement targets; manager check-ins every 2 weeks
- Outcomes: Successful completion (return to good standing) or separation
- PIPs are developed jointly by the manager, HR Business Partner, and employee

---

## 10. Promotions

Promotions are not tied exclusively to the annual review cycle. Managers may recommend a promotion at any time. Requirements:
- Minimum 12 months in current role (exceptions require VP approval)
- Performance rating of 4 or 5 in the most recent review
- Demonstrated readiness for next level (skill matrix available from HR)
- HR Business Partner and Compensation team approval
